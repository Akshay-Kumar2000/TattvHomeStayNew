Solang Valley

Solang Valley derives its name from combination of words Solang and Nallah. It is a side valley at the top of the Kullu Valley in Himachal Pradesh, India 14 km northwest of the resort town Manali on the way to Rohtang Pass, and is known for its summer and winter sport conditions. - Solang Valley is one of the most essential and popular destinations of NearbyAttractions in the itinerary of travellers who are visiting Himachal, in fact for adventure enthusiasts, there are lots of activities for all age groups and travellers, such as paragliding to parachuting, horse riding to driving mini open jeeps, snow scooters.

Rohtang Pass

When planning for a trip to Manali the ultimate and most essential day off in itinerary is the vista point Rohtang Pass - the place is located 50 kilometers away from the main town at a height of 3978 meters, one can only reach the place through road. The way up hills is a treat to eyes, as the vehicle climbs higher on the Manali - Keylong road, the picturesque view of the landscape around will leave you awestruck. There is a peculiar fact behind the name of the place, many of the CBRE workers died while attempting to cross the treacherous stretch, owing to its splendid natural beauty the place is famous among the community of film, many blockbusters have been shot here. It's an absolute bliss to gaze the beauty of nature in its rawest form possible, magnificent and sprawling landscape view, mountain peaks covered with snow and sonorous silence is a mesmerising thing worth experiencing.

Beas Kund Trek

Apart from other tourist destinations, Manali is also a way to one of the most undertaken treks – Beas Kund Trek, the trek is a perfect weekend getaway which will take you away from the loud and lousy crowd of city. Your eyes will get a chance to feast on an outstanding view of the Pir Pinjal mountain ranges over the Beas River, the spectacular meadows of Dhundi and Bakarthach and the glacier lake at Beas kund is worth all the hiking. So if you are planning to take a break from your otherwise monotonous life, then Beas Kund Trek is the right thing that you need. So mark your calendar, pack your bags and set off on the trek to let your soul rejuvenate.

Hadimba Temple

Hadimba Devi Temple, locally known as Dhungari Temple, also known variously as the Hadimba Temple, is located in the midst of the snow-covered hills of Manali.Hadimba temple is surrounded by beautiful cedar forests, the shrine is built on a rock which is believed to portray the image of Goddess Hadimba herself. It is an ancient cave temple dedicated to Hidimbi Devi, wife of Bhima, a figure in the Indian epic Mahabharata.

The Great Himalayan National Park

Located in the Kullu region of Himachal Pradesh The Great Himalayan National Park is India's one of the natural parks that is surrounded by the himalayan mountains from three sides and is a home to diverse wildlife species. The place received the title of national park in the year 1999 and at present is a home to 375 species of fauna, 31 species of mammals and 181 species of birds and the most attractive view of this national park are it's Deodar and Oak trees. The cloistered location of the park has made sure that the villages inside the park have a culture and traditions of their own, in fact every village has its own deity. For all nature lovers out there, The great himalayan national park is the most amazing place to relax and unwind away from the hustle bustle of city life, you can also be a part of different fairs organised in the month of April, May, August and September.

Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib

Manikaran Sahib is a famous pilgrimage destination for Sikhs and Hindus, located at a distance of 4 km from Kasol in the Parvati valley along the banks of River Parvati in the Kullu district of Himachal Pradesh. The place attracts a large number of travellers as the most vibrant thing to experience there is the hot water springs that are divided into three sections which are owned by Gurudwara and the guest houses. These hot springs hold a very spiritual and religious belief in the hearts of people, it’s believed that the water contains sulphur which has capabilities of curing diseases. The most fascinating fact about the hot spring is, the water is hot enough to cook food and so the gurdwara places the vessels in the hot kund and then serve the food in langar to all the travellers.

Hampta Pass

One of the most convenient and enjoyable treks is situated in the Manali region of Himachal Pradesh, which is widely known as Hampta Pass. The trek is laden with pristine green beauty of nature and comprises of dense pine forests, pristine glacial valleys, vast meadows and panoramic landscape. Travellers who wants to explore trekking or are beginners in trekking then this pass is the perfect choice as the place is easy to access and the adventurous experience of crossing the pass is simply exhilarating. Unlike most of the treks, Hampta Pass Trek begins from Manali town and not from a remote village area. There you can visit Chandratal – a crystal clear lake perched at a higher altitude is the most highlighting point of the trip and a rare breathtaking view to behold. So in the laps of the most mesmerising Himalayan mountain ranges, Hampta pass is a beautiful trek route for 4-5 days that will give you exciting and awesome vacation experience.

Naggar Castle

Found deep within the town of Manali, be awed by the complex architecture of Naggar Castle. This archaic castle constructed with a mixture of Himalayan and European materials and styles, features stunning mountain view. Relish in the sophisticated chamber s, classy domes and high walls, exquisite courtyards, and glance at the regal history of the territory. Great place for people who love history as well as who are hunting for great views!

Solang Valley

Located in the beautiful valley of Manali, Jogini waterfalls is about 3 kms away from the bustling town and around 2 kms away from the famous vashisht temple, the thing that makes this place more popular tourist spot is the trek from vashisht temple through pine trees and orchards, to the mesmerising fall cascading from a height of 160 feet. For nature lovers and adventure seekers the place is breathtaking valley in manali that offers trekkers enchanting experience of crossing different small streams, dazzling view of beas river and snow dusted mountain peaks of rohtang pass. The trek will take a minimum of three hours to complete so don't forget to carry your camera for capturing beautiful views, for pit – stop there are many restaurants in the way where you can sit back and enjoy a hot cup of tea. The waterfall is considered as the most romantic attraction spot in Manali, thronged by couples, the place is widely known for its adventure and recreational activities. All you need to do is pack your bags, essentials and trekking gear to keep going..!!

Vashisht Temple

Vashisht temple is a very famous and pious holy Vashisht temple is a very famous and pious holy destination in Manali that is widely known as vashisht baths. Vashisht baths is a hot water spring that is believed to possess spiritual powers and medicinal values, also known as vashisht springs the place is visited by lots of tourists, many of them take a dip in the holy water spring to get themselves rid of skin diseases and infections. Vashisht baths is situated 6 km away from Manali and is accompanied by beautiful sandstone temples and the government has made arrangements for separate bathing areas for men and women. The area around the vashisht hot spring is surrounded by hundreds of small shops where you can buy souvenirs and religious merchandise.


Kalath Hot Water

While following the course of the Beas river downstream for about 6 kilometers you will reach an obscure village called Kalath, the place is rich in sulphur containing hot water springs with curative properties of healing various diseases and in total there are six hot water springs in the area. The place has a very fascinating story of it evolvement and has two separate bathing areas for men and women.
